Updated on 2025-05-25

It is July 21st, and more than two months have passed. Two months ago, after comparing various GUI frameworks, I decided to temporarily give up flutter, and have been thinking about egui afterward.

Since asynchronous waiting operations are not allowed in the loop of egui, channels must be used to transfer data. When an event occurs, the app retains the receiving channel, creates a thread in the background to process the data, and sends the data to the app through the channel after processing. Each time the app loops, it checks the receiving channel data and updates the interface after receiving the data.

When trying to integrate simple asynchronous operations into egui, it is also necessary to solve the problem of multi-threaded shared resources. Use Arc<Mutex<T>> to share data; if read and write operations are performed simultaneously in multiple threads, RwLock<T> needs to be used to share data.

In addition, code reuse and structure also need to be considered. Use the App trait of egui to define the structure of the application, and use egui::Context to manage status and events. Recently, I found a project for reference, hoping that it will be helpful for subsequent development.
